【中英双语】中国如何将11座城整合为一座超级都市？

 t0ur1st
2025-11-02 约 6600 字 预计阅读 14 分钟 

最近在哔哩哔哩刷到了这个从Youtube搬运来的中英字幕视频《中国如何将11座城整合为一座超级都市！》，视频原标题为How China is Turning 11 Cities into One Giant Super City。

我个人觉得挺有意思，就把视频的中英字幕整理成markdown文档作为素材。我认为，一个国家，两种制度，三种货币，三个独立关税区，这是粤港澳大湾区的独特优势之一，其注定成为世界级湾区。

China has a plan worth nearly one trillion dollars, not for a single skyscraper or a new railway line, but to merge nine mainland cities, plus Hong Kong and Macau, into one connected giant super city. It sits on China’s southern coast, around the mouth of the Pearl River Delta.

中国正在推进一项价值近万亿美元的计划，不是用于建造一座摩天大楼或修建一条新铁路线，而是要将九个内地城市加上香港和澳门，融合成一个互联互通的巨型超级城市群。它坐落在中国南部沿海地区，环绕着珠江三角洲入海口。

On the map, it looks like a cluster of cities packed close together Hong Kong, Macau, Guangzhou, Shenzhen and several others. But the Chinese government doesn’t see them as separate dots. It sees them as pieces of a larger puzzle that, once fitted together, could form one of the most powerful metropolitan regions on Earth.

在地图上，它看起来像一个密集的城市群，香港、澳门、广州、深圳和其他几个城市。但中国政府并不认为它们是单独的点，而是看作一个更大拼图的组成部分，一旦拼合起来，就可能形成全球最具影响力的大都市圈之一。

The numbers give you a sense of scale. This region covers about 56,000 square kilometers, roughly the size of West Virginia in the United States or twice the size of Wales in the UK.

这些数字能让你对它的规模有个概念。该区域占地面积约5.6万平方公里，大致相当于美国西弗吉尼亚州的面积，或是英国威尔士面积的两倍。

Inside that area live more than 70 million people. That’s more than the population of France.

这片地区居住着7000多万人口。这个数字甚至比法国总人口还要多。

All concentrated in a coastal zone, you can cross by train in just a couple of hours. China is calling this the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macau Greater Bay Area. The name is not random.

所有一切都集中在沿海地区，你乘坐火车仅需几个小时就能横穿。中国将这一区域称为“粤港澳大湾区”，这个名字并非随意取的。

It’s modeled after other global hubs like Tokyo Bay or San Francisco Bay, both known for combining multiple cities into powerful economic engines. The goal is to make China’s Bay Area compete on the same stage, and possibly surpass them.

该规划借鉴了东京湾、旧金山湾等全球性枢纽城市的经验，这些地区都以将多个城市整合成强大经济引擎而闻名。其目标是让中国大湾区在同一舞台上竞争，甚至可能超越这些国际标杆。

Economically, the Greater Bay Area already produces around two trillion dollars of GDP a year, putting it on par with entire countries like Canada or Italy. So what exactly makes this cluster of cities so important, and why spend so much to tie them together?

从经济角度看，粤港澳大湾区每年已经创造出约2万亿美元的GDP，使其与加拿大或意大利等整个国家不相上下。那么，这个城市群究竟为何如此重要，又为什么要投入巨资将它们连接起来呢？

That’s where we need to start, by looking at what’s actually inside this region and what each city brings to the table. The Greater Bay Area includes nine mainland cities in Guangdong province, plus the two special administrative regions of Hong Kong and Macau.

我们应当从这个区域的实际状况着手，分析各城市对粤港澳大湾区的贡献。粤港澳大湾区包括广东省九个内地城市，以及香港和澳门两个特别行政区。

Individually, these cities are already strong. Shenzhen is a Tech powerhouse, home to HUAWEI, Tencent and DJI. Hong Kong is one of the world’s top financial centers. Guangzhou has been a major trading hub for centuries. But on their own, they can’t match the combined strength of global regions like Tokyo Bay, New York or Silicon Valley.

单独来看，这些城市各自实力雄厚：深圳是科技重镇，汇聚了华为、腾讯和大疆等科技巨头；香港是全球顶级金融中心之一；广州作为重要贸易枢纽已有数百年历史。但单凭其本身而言，它们仍难以匹敌东京湾、纽约或硅谷等全球顶级都市圈的综合实力。

China’s leaders believe that if you tie these cities together economically, physically and socially, you get an integrated region that can compete directly with the biggest hubs on the planet. In other words, it’s not just about bigger numbers, it’s about making the whole region operate like one giant economic machine, instead of a collection of smaller engines.

中国领导人坚信，若将这些城市在经济、基建和社会等多层面紧密联结，形成深度融合的都市圈，就能打造出一个与全球顶级枢纽一决高下的完整区域。说白了，这不仅是规模扩张的问题，更是要让整个区域像一台巨型经济引擎般协同运转，而非零散引擎的简单集合。

There’s also a strategic angle. Hong Kong and Macau still operate under the one country, two systems model with their own currencies, laws and borders. By physically and economically linking them to mainland cities like Shenzhen and Guangzhou, Beijing hopes to ensure their long term growth is tied more closely to the mainlands.

其中还有一个战略考量。香港和澳门仍实行一国两制，拥有各自的货币、法律和边界。通过将港澳与深圳、广州等内地城市在物理和经济上紧密联系，北京方面希望港澳的长期发展能与内地密不可分。

It’s a way of keeping the region stable and competitive at the same time. And then there’s efficiency. Without coordination, each city builds its own airports, ports, industrial parks and Tech zones. By planning together, they can share resources instead of duplicating them.

这是保持该地区稳定并提升竞争力的一种方式。其次是效率考量。如果缺乏协调，每个城市都会建设各自的机场、港口、工业园区和科技园区。通过共同规划，他们可以共享资源，而非重复建设。

A factory in Dongguan might be supplied by Tech developed in Shenzhen, financed by Hong Kong, and shipped through Guangzhou, all without friction if the system works as intended.

东莞的工厂可能采用深圳研发的技术，由香港提供资金，并通过广州出海运输，如果系统按预期运行，这一切都将畅通无阻。

The vision is simple to state. Make the Greater Bay Area a supercharged economic zone that rivals the world’s most advanced regions.

这个愿景简明扼要：将粤港澳大湾区打造成一个与全球顶尖地区比肩的超级经济区。

The challenge is turning that idea into a functioning reality. And that’s where history comes in. Because this plan has been in motion for more than a decade, the roots of the plan go back more than 15 years.

真正的挑战在于如何将这一构想转化成现实。这就涉及到历史因素了。由于该计划已实施十余年，其历史渊源可追溯至十五年前。

In 2008, China’s central planners floated a bold idea, merge the nine mainland cities of the Pearl River Delta into one vast urban cluster. At the time, it sounded ambitious, even unrealistic.

2008年，中国党中央决策层提出一个大胆的想法，将珠江三角洲的九个城市合并成一个庞大的城市群。在当时，这个想法听起来雄心勃勃，甚至不切实际。

But it wasn’t just talk the government started. The government started mapping out how to integrate transport, industry, and resources across the region.

但这并不只是空谈。政府开始规划如何整合该地区的交通、产业与资源。

By the mid-2010s, the concept had grown into a national priority.

到2010年代中期，这一构想已上升为国家战略重点工作。

In 2017, Guangdong province, Hong Kong and Macau signed agreements to coordinate their development strategies.

2017年，广东省与香港、澳门签署了区域协调发展战略的协议。

The following year, the State Council gave the project its official name, the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macau Greater Bay Area. Then came the turning point. In February 2019, Beijing released the Outline Development Plan, the master document that spelled out what the GBA should look like by 2035.

次年，国务院正式将该项目命名为粤港澳大湾区。随后迎来关键转折——2019年2月，北京方面发布《粤港澳大湾区发展规划纲要》，这份纲领性文件明确了粤港澳大湾区到2035年的发展蓝图。

It covered everything. A connected high-speed transport network, shared innovation hubs and research institutions, coordinated ports and airports, stronger ties between Hong Kong’s finance and Shenzhen’s technology.

内容涵盖方方面面。互联互通的高速运输网络，共享的创新中心和科研机构，协调发展的港口和机场，以及香港金融与深圳科技之间更加紧密的联系。

The blueprint also identified more than 150 major infrastructure projects, with early estimates putting the price tag at over two trillion yuan, around 320 billion dollars. Over time, that figure grew. And when you add up all the planned projects, investments and new zones, the total is expected to approach the one trillion dollar mark.

该规划还确定了150多个重大基础设施项目，初步估算成本超过2万亿元，约合3200亿美元。随着时间的推移，这个数字持续增长。如果将所有规划项目、投资和新区的投入加在一起，预计总额将接近1万亿美元大关。

What sounded like a futuristic vision in 2008 has now become a structured roadmap backed by laws, budgets and construction crews. And the next step in understanding the Greater Bay Area is looking at the cities themselves, the players that make up this massive merger.

2008年听起来像是未来愿景的构想，如今已发展成为有法律支撑、预算规划和施工团队的系统化蓝图。而要深入理解粤港澳大湾区的发展脉络，下一步就要聚焦于这些城市本身——正是它们共同构成了这场宏大的区域整合。

To understand what this mega city really means, you have to look at the players that make it up.

要真正理解这座超级城市的意义，必须观察其构成要素。

Hong Kong about 7.5 million people, one of the world’s top financial hubs, and a city that operates on its own legal system and its own currency. It’s where international capital flows in and out of China, and that gives the Greater Bay Area a global edge.

香港约有750万人口，是全球顶级金融中心之一，也是一个拥有独立法律体系和货币制度的城市。这里是国际资本进出中国的门户，这也赋予粤港澳大湾区全球竞争优势。

Macau much smaller at 700,000 people, but it’s a heavyweight in tourism and casinos. Beijing wants Macau to diversify into Tech and finance, and being tied into the Bay Area makes that easier.

澳门人口规模虽小仅有70万，但在旅游业和博彩业却举足轻重。北京方面希望澳门能向科技和金融领域多元化发展，而与大湾区的紧密联系使这一切更容易实现。

Guangzhou with roughly 15 million residents, it’s the historic trading heart of southern China. For centuries, it’s been the gateway for goods coming in and out of China, and today it’s still a manufacturing and commerce powerhouse.

广州拥有约1500万居民，是华南地区历史悠久的商贸中心。几个世纪以来，它一直是货物进出中国的门户，如今依然是制造业和商业的重要枢纽。

Shenzhen around 13 million people now live here. 40 years ago, it was a fishing village. Today, it’s China’s Silicon Valley, home to HUAWEI, Tencent, DJI and thousands of startups. It’s where most of the region’s Tech innovation happens.

深圳目前居住着大约1300万人。40年前，这里还只是个小渔村。如今，它已成为中国的硅谷，汇聚了HUAWEI、腾讯、大疆等科技巨头和数千家初创企业。这里是该地区大部分科技创新的发源地。

Dongguan, the industrial engine. Its factories supply the world with electronics, clothing and machinery. Many of the parts in your phone or laptop probably passed through Dongguan at some stage.

东莞，工业引擎。这里的工厂为全球供应电子产品、服装和机械设备。你手机或笔记本电脑中的许多零部件，很可能曾在东莞经过了某个生产流程。

Foshan, famous for manufacturing too, from ceramics to appliances and part of Guangzhou’s metropolitan area, it’s also known in pop culture, as the home of Bruce Lee’s martial arts heritage.

佛山也以制造业而闻名，从陶瓷制品到家用电器，属于广州大都市圈的一部分，它在流行文化中也享有盛名，是李小龙武术遗产的故乡。

Zhuhai, a coastal city right next to Macau, known for tourism, some Tech, and increasingly for being a bridge, literally and figuratively, between the mainland and Macau.

珠海这座毗邻澳门的沿海城市，以旅游业和部分科技产业著称，并因其在地理与精神层面架起的桥梁枢纽而日益闻名。

Huizhou, a growing industrial hub with electronics factories and petrochemical industries, playing a quieter but important supporting role.

惠州作为电子制造和石化产业的新兴工业中心，扮演着虽低调却发挥着重要支撑作用的角色。

Zhongshan, known for light manufacturing, especially in electronics and lighting, and historically famous as the hometown of Sun Yat-sen, the founding father of modern China.

中山以轻工业制造著称，尤其是在电子和照明领域，并且因为是现代中国奠基人孙中山先生的故乡而闻名。

Jiangmen and Zhaoqing, smaller compared to the giants, but both are expanding, with industries ranging from paper and textiles to new energy and logistics.

江门和肇庆，虽然规模不及那些巨头，但都在不断持续发展，产业范围从造纸纺织到新能源和物流。

Together, these 11 cities form a region with enormous diversity. Finance, Tech, tourism, factories, and trade ports all sitting side by side. And when you link those roles together, you get an economy that’s far more powerful than the sum of its parts.

这11座城市共同构成了一个极具多样性的区域。金融、科技、旅游、工厂和贸易港口等产业在次并存。当把这些功能连接在一起时，就形成了一个整体效应将远超各部分相加之和的强大经济体。

But merging cities on paper is one thing. To actually make them feel like one connected place, you need massive physical infrastructure, and that’s where China has already started building on a scale few countries can match. Infrastructure is the backbone of the entire plan.

但纸上谈兵地合并城市是一回事。要真正让它们形成一个互联的整体，需要大规模的基础设施建设作为支撑，而基建正是中国已经发展到鲜有国家能及其规模之大的领域。基础设施建设是整个计划的支柱。

One of the flagship projects is the Hong Kong-Zhuhai-Macau bridge. At 55 kilometers long, it’s the world’s longest sea-crossing bridge. Opened in 2018 at a cost of over 18 billion dollars, It cut travel time between Hong Kong and Zhuhai from about four hours to under one.

港珠澳大桥是其中的旗舰项目之一。这座全长55公里的大桥是世界上最长的跨海桥梁。港珠澳大桥于2018年通车，耗资超过180亿美元，将香港至珠海的通勤时间从约四小时缩短至不到一小时。

Then there’s the Guangzhou-Shenzhen-Hong Kong express rail link. This high speed rail corridor connects Hong Kong directly to China’s national high speed rail grid. A trip from Hong Kong to Guangzhou, which used to take two to three hours, now takes just 47 minutes.

还有广深港高速铁路的开通，这条高铁走廊将香港直接接入中国国家高速铁路网络。从香港到广州的行程，原本需要两到三个小时，现在仅需47分钟即可完成。

That effectively shrinks the region, making daily cross-border commuting a realistic option.

这些举措有效缩小了区域距离，使日常跨境通勤成为现实选择。

Beyond these headline projects, the government has mapped out more than 150 major infrastructure projects. These include new expressways crisscrossing the delta, expanded metro systems linking cities like Shenzhen and Dongguan, and coordinated AirPort strategies between Guangzhou, Hong Kong and Shenzhen, so they complement rather than compete with each other.

除这些重点项目外，政府还规划了150多个重大基础设施项目，包括纵横交错的珠三角新高速公路，连接深圳和东莞等城市的地铁系统扩建工程，以及广州、香港与深圳之间协调发展的机场战略——这些规划旨在形成互补而非相互竞争。

The goal is to build what officials call a one-hour living circle, a network where any two points in the Greater Bay Area can be reached within an hour by fast transport. All of this construction is transforming the Pearl River Delta into a single tightly knit metro area.

目标是打造官员们所说的“一小时生活圈”——即大湾区内任意两点都能在一小时内到达的快速交通网络。所有这些建设正在将珠江三角洲打造成紧密相连的大都市圈。

But the Greater Bay Area is about more than concrete and steel. To really merge 11 different jurisdictions, China also needs to integrate economies, policies and even daily life.

但大湾区的融合远不止于钢筋水泥。要真正实现11个不同地区的深度融合，中国还需要在经济、政策乃至日常生活等层面实施整合。

Bridges and trains may link the cities physically, but it isn’t meant to be just a transport project. To work as a single region, the economies, policies and even people’s daily lives also need to connect.

桥梁与铁路或许能实现城市间的物理连接，但这不仅仅是个交通项目。要作为一个整体区域运作，经济、政策乃至民众日常生活也需要紧密相连。

One example is the Qianhai Cooperation Zone in Shenzhen. It’s a special district where Hong Kong and Shenzhen businesses are encouraged to partner, combining Hong Kong’s financial expertise with Shenzhen’s technology and manufacturing.

深圳的前海合作区就是一个范例。这个特别区域鼓励香港与深圳的企业合作，将香港的金融专长与深圳的科技制造优势相互结合。

On the other side of the Delta, Hengqin island is being developed to help Macau branch out beyond casinos into finance, health care and research, supported by nearby Zhuhai.

在珠三角的另一端，横琴岛正在开发中，以帮助澳门在珠海的支持下从博彩业拓展到金融、医疗保健和研究领域。

There are also steps to make working across borders easier. Hong Kong and Macau residents can now apply for residence permits in mainland cities, giving them access to schools, health care and housing. Cross-border travel has been simplified with new permits and commuter passes.

还有一些措施使跨境工作更加便利。港澳居民现在可以申请内地城市的居住证，让他们能够享受教育、医疗和住房服务。跨境旅行也由于新许可证和通行证的推出而简化。

In finance, programs like the Stock Connect and Wealth Management Connect allow investors in Hong Kong, Macau and Guangdong to move money and investments more freely across the region.

在金融领域，股票通和理财通等项目允许香港、澳门和广东的投资者能够更自由地在该区域内转移资金和投资。

Education and research are part of the plan, too. Universities in Hong Kong, Shenzhen and Guangzhou have launched joint laboratories and student programs. Hospitals are testing ways to share medical records across borders, so a patient in Macau could get treatment in a Shenzhen clinic more seamlessly.

教育和科研也是规划的一部分。香港、深圳和广州的大学已联合建设实验室和学生交流项目。医院正在测试跨境共享医疗记录的方式，让澳门的患者在深圳的诊所就医时更加顺畅。

These changes may sound technical, but they are what make the Greater Bay Area more than just a set of highways. They turn 11 jurisdictions into a region where talent, capital and ideas can move almost as freely as within a single city.

这些改革听起来很具有技术性，但正是它们让粤港澳大湾区不仅仅是一系列高速公路的集合体，而是将11个行政区整合为有机整体，打造一个让人才、资本与创意如同在单一城市般自由流动的区域。

Of course, all of this costs money. And the next question is whether the nearly one trillion dollars being poured into this project is actually worth it. And what kind of economic return China expects?

当然，所有这一切都需要资金投入。接下来的问题是，投入这个项目的近1万亿美元是否真的值得，以及中国期望获得怎样的经济回报。

The Greater Bay Area already produces an economy of about two trillion US dollars each year. If you treated it as a country, it would rank among the world’s top 10 economies. And that’s before the integration is even complete.

粤港澳大湾区每年已经产生约2万亿美元的经济总量。如果将其视为一个国家，其经济规模将跻身全球十大经济体之列——而这还是在区域一体化尚未完成的情况下。

By 2030, projections show the GBA could reach a GDP of 2.5 to 3 trillion dollars, with a population climbing toward 80 million people.

预计到2030年，粤港澳大湾区（GBA）的GDP可能达到2.5至3万亿美元，人口规模也将攀升至8000万。

That would make it one of the largest urban economies on Earth, putting it in the same league as the Tokyo Bay Area, long considered the world’s most powerful metro region.

这将使其成为全球最大的都市经济体之一，与长期以来被视为世界最强大都市区的东京湾区齐名。

The logic is simple. Better infrastructure and unified policies mean less wasted time and more efficient trade.

其逻辑很简单：更完善的基础设施和统一的政策意味着更少的时间浪费和更高效的贸易。

If a company can design a product in Shenzhen, finance it in Hong Kong, manufacture it in Dongguan, and ship it out of Guangzhou, all in a single day. That kind of speed directly translates to economic growth.

如果一家公司可以在深圳设计产品，在香港融资，在东莞制造，再从广州发货，所有这些全部都在一天内完成。这种速度能直接转化为经济增长。

Of course, such massive investment isn’t just about GDP. It’s also about long-term positioning.

当然，如此大规模的投资不仅关乎GDP，更关乎长远战略布局。

For China, the Greater Bay Area is a way to anchor global finance, technology and manufacturing in one integrated region. It’s part economic bet, part strategic insurance.

对中国而言，粤港澳大湾区是将全球金融、科技和制造业整合在一个综合区域的绝佳平台。这既是经济赌注，也是战略保障。

The bigger question now is what it means for the rest of the world.

现在更大的问题是，这对世界其他地区意味着什么。

If China can pull this off, the Greater Bay Area could become a new center of global finance, technology and manufacturing, redefining what we mean by a city in the 21st century.

如果中国能够成功实现这一愿景，粤港澳大湾区或将成为全球金融、技术和制造业的新中心，重新定义21世纪城市的概念。

If it struggles, it will stand as a warning about the limits of large-scale integration.

若其遭遇困境，将作为大规模融合项目局限性的警示，揭示大规模一体化的边界所在。

Either way, the world will be watching closely. Because what happens in the Greater Bay Area won’t just change southern China, it could reshape the global urban map.

无论如何，世界都将密切关注粤港澳大湾区的发展动向。因为粤港澳大湾区的发展动向不仅将改变华南地区，更可能重塑全球城市格局。
